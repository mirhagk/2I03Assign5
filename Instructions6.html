Written Assignment

Write a report of 4 - 8 pages — brevity is valued but depending on the topic, a longer report may be more adequate. The report has to have an introduction, main part, conclusions, and must cite all references. The first page should state the title of the report and all group members. The introduction, half a page to a page, should give a background that orients the reader, establish the need for the report by revealing a gap or problem worth addressing, show how the report fills the gap by stating the specific purpose, and provide an overview of the document’s organization; see the course notes on Engineering Communication. The main part has first to introduce the topic in sufficient depth and then compare the approaches or tools. When comparing X to Y, the state the criteria for comparison, discuss what is similar and what is different, and why so. The conclusion, of half a page to a page, draws consequences from the comparison, for example, why one would prefer one over the other, when one would prefer one over the other, without repeating the main part. Use credible arguments and rhetorical patterns as in the course notes on Engineering Communication. A report that simply rephrases material on X and Y without setting them properly in relation is not satisfactory. The report will be judged on the language, how well it is typeset, adequacy of figures, clarity of explanations, proper structure, conciseness and convincingness of arguments, adequacy of references.
